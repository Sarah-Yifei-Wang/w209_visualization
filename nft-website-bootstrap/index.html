An Introduction to Non-Fungible Tokens

NFTs are digital assets that represent real-world objects like art, music, in-game items, and videos.
The ownership of this digital collectible is recorded on a blockchain that cannot be replicated.
Owning an NFT is analogous to owning an original physical piece of art.
NFTs can be bought and sold in different online Marketplaces and are primarily purchased with cryptocurrencies.

NFT Sales vs. Transactions


To get a general understanding of NFT markets, there are thousands of NFT collections out there. What are the popular ones? When looking at the relationship between Sales and Transactions, does a large number of transactions necessarily imply large sales volume?

NFT Popularity


NFTs are like physical collectors items, only digital. They have exclusive ownership rights. Because NFT ownership is easy to certify and transfer, we would like to explore the relationship between collections and their ownership.

First, we visualize where potential NFT market participants come from. Because trading is performed using cryptocurrencies and virtual wallets (“owner addresses”), the exact location of trades is impossible to record. Therefore, we examine Google search trends of “NFT” keywords to obtain a general sense of the location of the market participants.




NFT Owners and Collections


Because NFT ownership is easy to certify, it would also be useful to investigate if there is a broad base of NFT owners. In other words, is the ownership democratic, in which many people own few NFTs, or is there a smaller group of people that own most of them?



CryptoPunks: How may we quantify the uniqueness of an NFT within a collection?

Now that you’ve seen general NFT trends comparing transactions, sales, marketplaces, and owners, let’s look more closely at a single collection. In particular, let’s try to figure out how one individual NFT token within a collection may compare to another in the same collection. What makes one more valuable than another?


We focus our attention to the CryptoPunks collection, created in June 2017 by Larva Labs studio, making it one of the oldest NFT collections to exist. The CryptoPunks collections contains 10,000 NFTs, each equipped with a set of unique attributes.

In some cases, the attributes of an NFT are randomly generated and distributed throughout the collection. Some attributes may be seen more frequently among the tokens, and others are considered to be very unique. This is the case for the CryptoPunks NFT collection.

CryptoPunks, in particular, are associated with two attributes: the Punk Type, and the Punk Accessories. Each Punk has one Type and between zero and seven Accessories. An overview of these counts is shown in the visualization below.


We’d like to investigate whether Punks associated with more unique Types or more unique Accessories sell for more, on average.

A uniqueness score was computed based on the accessory type frequencies for each of the Punks. Scoring was computed using term-frequency-inverse document frequency (tf-idf), a common measure that quantifies the significance of categorical data. In the context of our problem, the higher the score, the more unique the NFT.

The next chart shows Punks that fall in the top 5% of average sales price, plotted against their uniqueness score.
