An Introduction to Non-Fungible Tokens


Despite how familiar you are with the concept, it is likely that you have heard the acronym, NFT, which stands for Non-Fungible Token. While NFTs have been around since 2017, when Larva Labs developed the famous CryptoPunks Collection, 2021 marked a dramatic spike in sales. This is not an exaggeration — NFT sales hit $17.6 billion in 2021, an increase of 21,000% since 2020’s sales of $82 million. In fact, the most expensive NFT ever sold was auctioned off in 2021 - a $69 million sale by digital artist Beeple, which is shown below.


While the concept of NFTs may not be immediately intuitive, the purpose of this website is to help you visualize and explore the NFT universe through different perspectives, hoping you may grasp a better understanding of the concept. After viewing our visualization project, we hope you might think about whether or not you would like to own an NFT yourself.

What is an NFT?

An NFT is a digital asset that links ownership with a unique digital (but sometimes physical) item, such as works of art, collectibles, music, videos or virtual avatars.


An NFT is the unique identifier that certifies the digital item is the true one. Each NFT is securely stored on a blockchain - the same technology behind cryptocurrencies - which ensures the asset is one-of-a-kind. Blockchain technology makes it nearly impossible to alter or counterfeit NFTs.

For example, the CryptoKitties NFT collection, is a digital trading game on the cryptocurrency platform Ethereum that allows people to purchase and sell unique virtual cats stored on the blockchain.


An NFT is just a digital good that can be “tokenized”, or “uniquely identified”, making it almost impossible to replicate, and can range from assets such as artwork, items in a game, photos, or a video from a live broadcast.

How do you buy or sell an NFT?

NFTs can be purchased in an online marketplace, a platform that host thousands of NFT creators and collectors. Some of the most common NFT marketplaces include OpenSea, Mintable, Nifty Gateway and Rarible, as well as niche marketplaces for more specific types of NFTs (e.g. NBA Top Shots).

Since the majority of NFT marketplaces only accept transactions in cryptocurrency, a buyer must first create a cryptocurrency wallet before purchasing an NFT. This wallet is a program that interacts with the blockchain and allows users to store and retrieve their digital assets. Each wallet has a sequence of letters and numbers called an owner address that uniquely identifies the owner. While most NFTs are purchased using Ethereum (“ETH”), NFTs can also be bought with other crypto such as WAX and Flow.

The next section defines some of the common terms that we will be using throughout the website. Familiarizing yourself with these terms will facilitate your understanding of the visuals. Enjoy the exploration!

Common Terms


Below are some common terms that we will use throughout our exploration.

Collection: A group of NFTs that share the same characteristics and are typically from the same artist(s)
- e.g. CryptoKitties, Bored Ape Yacht Club, Art Blocks

Token and Token ID: An NFT in a collection may be called a token, and each token is associated with an ID.
- e.g. The CryptoPunks Collection has 10,000 tokens, and each token ID is a number between 0 and 9999.

Marketplace: Any website that facilitates the buying and selling of NFTs
- e.g. OpenSea, Mintable

Owner Address: A sequence of letters and numbers that represents the unique crypto wallet address book that contains a set of secret numbers, or private keys
- e.g. bc1qxy2kgdygjrsqtzq2n0yrf2493p83kkfjhx0wlh

Number of Transactions: The number of NFTs that are sold in a marketplace

Sales Value: The total price transferred from an NFT transaction. Most NFT sales are reported in ETH, but can be converted to USD based on ETH price

Cryptocurrency: A digital currency that uses blockchain technology to verify and maintain records of transactions
- e.g. Ethereum, Bitcoin, Litecoin

NFT Popularity

First, we visualize where potential NFT market participants come from. Because trading is performed using cryptocurrencies and virtual wallets (“owner addresses”), the exact location of trades is difficult to record, and data from nearby servers is hard to obtain. Therefore, we examine worldwide Google Search trends of “NFT” keywords to obtain a general sense of where the NFT interests are the most dense.

One interesting insight is that China ranks the highest in terms of NFT Google Search, even though cryptocurrency was banned from trading back in September of 2021.



Next, we investigate the time series of the NFT Google Search trends across the top 20 regions, as well as worldwide.

The trends are largely similar across regions, and it is even surprising to see the recent plummeting of interest. It reignites the discussion about whether the concept of an NFT is a fad, or rather a true new form of digital assets.

CryptoPunks


CryptoPunks: Sales Price and Uniqueness

Now that you’ve seen general NFT trends comparing transactions, sales, marketplaces, and owners, let’s look more closely at a single collection. In particular, let’s try to figure out how one individual NFT token within a collection may compare to another in the same collection. What makes one more valuable than another?



We focus our attention to the CryptoPunks collection, created in June 2017 by Larva Labs studio, making it one of the oldest NFT collections to exist. The CryptoPunks collections contains 10,000 NFTs, each equipped with a set of unique attributes. Each Punk is associated with a Punk ID, a number between 0 and 9999.


In some cases, the attributes of an NFT are randomly generated and distributed throughout the collection. Some attributes may be seen more frequently among the tokens, and others are considered to be very unique. This is the case for the CryptoPunks NFT collection.


CryptoPunks, in particular, are associated with two attributes: the Punk Type, and the Punk Accessories. Each Punk has one Type and between zero and seven Accessories. An overview of these counts is shown in the visualization below. The bottom right histogram shows that most Punks have between 2 and 3 accessories. Can you determine the most popular accessories and types? How about the most unique?




Using the above data, we investigated whether Punks associated with more unique Types or more unique Accessories sell for more, on average.

A uniqueness score was computed based on the Accessory and Type frequencies for each of the Punks. Scoring was computed using term frequency-inverse document frequency (tf-idf), a common measure that quantifies the significance of categorical data. In the context of our problem, the higher the score, the more unique the NFT.

The next chart shows Punks that fall in the top 5% of average sales price (y-axis), plotted against their uniqueness score (x-axis). Notice that Punk 7804 in the top right corner of the chart seems to be both the highest selling NFT and the most unique NFT. Does this relationship seem to hold for all NFTs on this chart?

Hover over the NFTs to view their Punk ID, and zoom into the chart to get a better view of each Punk. You may also filter on Punk Type, if you're interested in comparing certain Types.
